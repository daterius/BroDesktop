# Just a free tool that works

BROdesktop is made by BRO.TJ. This is our manifesto — why it exists, what we believe, and why you can trust it.

## Why we built it

Everyone needs remote access: helping your parents with their PC, fixing a friend's laptop, supporting a client. But the usual apps are either expensive, cut the free version down to useless, or constantly nag you to pay.

At BRO.TJ we decided to do the opposite: a tool you just download — and it works. Free. No tricks.

## Our principles

**01 · Free and honest**  
No forced donations, subscriptions or hidden fees. It's free today, and we won't force you to pay. If support for the project ever appears, it will be voluntary only.

**02 · Your privacy**  
The connection goes directly between devices (P2P) and is AES-256 encrypted. We don't see your screen, don't record sessions and don't trade your data.

**03 · Protecting people**  
Remote access is often abused by scammers. That's why BROdesktop has built-in warnings before a connection, a blocklist of scammer IDs and one-click reporting.

**04 · Openness**  
We're honest about what's inside: BROdesktop is based on the open-source RustDesk and runs through our own servers. No lies about how it works.

**05 · Simplicity**  
Download and it works. You can start right away without signing up (with a daily time limit). A free sign-up removes the limit and unlocks the address book.

## How we're different

In short — why BROdesktop instead of AnyDesk or TeamViewer:

- **Free, not crippled** — After a free sign-up: no daily limit and no nagging pop-ups.
- **Our own server** — Traffic goes through our servers — lower latency, your data doesn't go to third parties.
- **Scam protection** — Built-in anti-fraud that ordinary apps don't have.
- **For everyone** — A multilingual interface and simple, readable IDs with a BRO prefix.

## Why it may look suspicious — and why it's all honest

We're a young project. The app doesn't yet have an expensive code-signing certificate, so on launch Windows shows “Windows protected your PC” and marks the publisher as unknown. That's how the system reacts to any new program without a paid certificate — it doesn't mean the app is dangerous.

We know trust has to be earned, so we say plainly what we do and don't do.

### What BROdesktop does NOT do

- ❌ Doesn't mine cryptocurrency or secretly load your computer.
- ❌ Doesn't track or spy on you.
- ❌ Doesn't show ads.
- ❌ Doesn't sell or share your data.

### How to check for yourself

- ✅ The connection is AES-256 encrypted and the server key is verified on every connection.
- ✅ You can scan the file with any antivirus or on VirusTotal.
- ✅ It's built on the open-source RustDesk; our site and contacts are public.

Over time we'll add a code-signing certificate and the warning will disappear.
